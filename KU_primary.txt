<h1>What is Open Access?</h1>

<p style="font-size:1.5em; line-height:1.5em">Open Access is an international movement that has the goal of making peer-reviewed published scholarship available free of charge to the public and to the global scholarly community.</p>

<p style="font-size:1.5em; line-height:1.5em">For additional reading about open access in general we recommend Wikipedia's well-referenced <a class="external" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_access">Open Access</a> article.</p>

<h2>KU Faculty's Open Access Policy</h2>

<p>The <a class="external" href="https://documents.ku.edu/policies/governance/OpenAccess.htm"> KU Faculty Open Access Policy</a> (revised and passed the faculty senate on February 11, 2010) is a mechanism that asserts the rights of KU faculty to provide broad, free access to their journal publications to colleagues around the world.</p>

<p>Under the policy, KU faculty can exercise these rights by retaining enough copyrights and once rights are retained provide KU with the necessary bibliographic information and a copy of the publication. KU then provides efficient and effective access to these publications by means of <a class="external" href="http://kuscholarworks.ku.edu/">KU ScholarWorks</a>.  If a publisher requires a faculty member to opt out of the policy for a specific article, the author may submit an <a href="https://openaccess.ku.edu/open-access-policy-waiver-form">Open Access Policy Waiver Form</a>.</p>
