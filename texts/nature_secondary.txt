<h1 class="small-space-below title-color strong line-below">About open access</h1><div><p>Nature Research publishes more than 30 fully open access journals and a further 40 with a hybrid open access option. 44 per cent of the research articles we published last year were open access. Our flagship journal, <em>Nature Communications</em>, is the most cited multidisciplinary open science journal in the world.</p>
<p>We keep our open access policies under constant review, while using a transparent approach to facilitating open science through data services, collaboration tools and public accessibility and reuse. In the last 12 months we’ve made great progress through launching <a href=http://www.nature.com/sdata/data-policies/repositories><em>Scientific Data</em></a>, <a href=http://www.palgrave-journals.com/palcomms/><em>Palgrave Communications</em></a>, <a href=http://www.nature.com/content/NPJ/index.html>Nature Partner Journals</a>, through switching <a href=http://www.nature.com/ncomms/index.html><em>Nature Communications</em></a> to full open access and by introducing CC BY 4.0 as default across all NPG-owned fully open access journals.</p>
